Can We Hear from Events?
Generating Speech from Neuromorphic Events

Abstract

Traditional visually-guided speech generation relies heavily on frame-based RGB inputs, yet it faces a fundamental physical bottleneck: the "Temporal Granularity Mismatch". Since standard cameras integrate light over a fixed exposure time, they inevitably blur the high-frequency articulatory transients (e.g., micro-tremors and rapid lip onsets) essential for expressive speech. To break this ceiling, we propose EventSpeech, the first framework to leverage neuromorphic events for expressive speech generation. Event sensors yield asynchronous, microsecond-precise streams that naturally align with the temporal dynamics of acoustic waveforms. Our approach features a multi-scale Audio Encoder synergizing the proposed Hierarchical Wavelet Contextualizer (HWC), coupled with a bidirectional alignment mechanism that synchronizes sparse visual dynamics with dense acoustic features. Crucially, to validate the superiority of events beyond simulation, we construct EVT-SPK, the first benchmark comprising both large-scale synthetic data and real-world recordings captured via specialized neuromorphic hardware. Extensive experiments demonstrate that EventSpeech significantly outperforms state-of-the-art across the objective and subjective evaluation, particularly in preserving fine-grained prosody and maintaining robustness against motion blur, establishing a new paradigm for speech generation based on neuromorphic events.
