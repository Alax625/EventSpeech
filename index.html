Can We Hear from Events? Generating Speech from Neuromorphic Events

Abstract

Traditional visual-guided speech generation relies heavily on frame-based RGB inputs, yet it faces a fundamental physical bottleneck: the "Tempo- ral Granularity Mismatch". Since standard cam- eras integrate light over a fixed exposure time, they inevitably blur the high-frequency articula- tory transients (e.g., micro-tremors and rapid lip onsets) essential for expressive speech. To break this ceiling, we propose EventSpeech, the first framework to leverage neuromorphic event sig- nals for high-fidelity speech generation. Unlike frames, event sensors asynchronously record in- tensity changes with microsecond precision, pro- viding a continuous kinematic stream that is struc- turally isomorphic to acoustic waveforms. Our approach features a multi-scale Audio Encoder synergizing Mamba and Wavelet transforms, cou- pled with a bidirectional alignment mechanism that synchronizes sparse visual dynamics with dense acoustic features. Crucially, to validate the superiority of events beyond simulation, we construct EVT-SPK, the first benchmark com- prising both large-scale synthetic data and real- world recordings captured via specialized neuro- morphic hardware. Extensive experiments demon- strate that EventSpeech significantly outperforms state-of-the-art RGB-based methods, particularly in preserving fine-grained prosody and maintain- ing robustness against motion blur, establishing a new paradigm for physically-grounded speech generation.
