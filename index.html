Can We Hear from Events? Generating Speech from Neuromorphic Events

Abstract

Traditional visual-guided speech generation relies heavily on frame-based RGB inputs, yet it faces a fundamental physical bottleneck: the "Temporal Granularity Mismatch". Since standard cameras integrate light over a fixed exposure time, they inevitably blur the high-frequency articulatory transients (e.g., micro-tremors and rapid lip onsets) essential for expressive speech. To break this ceiling, we propose EventSpeech, the first framework to leverage neuromorphic event signals for high-fidelity speech generation. Unlike frames, event sensors asynchronously record intensity changes with microsecond precision, providing a continuous kinematic stream that is structurally isomorphic to acoustic waveforms. Our approach features a multi-scale AudioEncoder synergizing Mamba and Wavelet transforms, coupled with a bidirectional alignment mechanism that synchronizes sparse visual dynamics with dense acoustic features. Crucially, to validate the superiority of events beyond simulation, we construct EVT-SPK, the first benchmark comprising both large-scale synthetic data and real-world recordings captured via specialized neuromorphic hardware. Extensive experiments demonstrate that EventSpeech significantly outperforms state-of-the-art RGB-based methods, particularly in preserving fine-grained prosody and maintaining robustness against motion blur, establishing a new paradigm for physically-grounded speech generation.
