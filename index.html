Can We Hear from Events?
Generating Speech from Neuromorphic Events

arXiv (Coming Soon) Dataset (Coming Soon)

Abstract

Traditional visually-guided speech generation relies heavily on frame-based RGB inputs, yet it faces a fundamental physical bottleneck: the "Temporal Granularity Mismatch". Since standard cameras integrate light over a fixed exposure time, they inevitably blur the high-frequency articulatory transients (e.g., micro-tremors and rapid lip onsets) essential for expressive speech. To break this ceiling, we propose EventSpeech, the first framework to leverage neuromorphic event signals for high-fidelity speech generation. Event sensors yield asynchronous, microsecond-precise streams structurally isomorphic to acoustic waveforms. Our approach features a multi-scale Audio Encoder synergizing the proposed Hierarchical Wavelet Contextualizer (HWC), coupled with a bidirectional alignment mechanism that synchronizes sparse visual dynamics with dense acoustic features. Crucially, to validate the superiority of events beyond simulation, we construct EVT-SPK, the first benchmark comprising both large-scale synthetic data and real-world recordings captured via specialized neuromorphic hardware. Extensive experiments demonstrate that EventSpeech significantly outperforms state-of-the-art RGB-based methods, particularly in preserving fine-grained prosody and maintaining robustness against motion blur, establishing a new paradigm for physically-grounded speech generation.

Method

Figure 2. Overview of the EventSpeech core architecture. Event Encoder (left): Transduces video into events via V2E, extracting disentangled kinematics (e.g., Lip Motion) using a MHFE. Audio Encoder (right): Synergizes Mamba blocks with HWC to capture both global prosody and local spectral details. Alignment & Generation (middle): Bridges the temporal gap via bidirectional attention and InfoNCE, conditioning the Flow Matching decoder for expressive speech generation.
